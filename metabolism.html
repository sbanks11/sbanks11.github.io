Did you know that after age 40, your body can become "metabolically rigid"—meaning it gets stuck burning sugar for energy instead of fat?

This isn't about a slow metabolism.

It's not about eating too much or exercising too little.

And it's definitely not about lacking willpower.

It's about your body being locked into the wrong fuel source.

Think about it like this: Your body is like a hybrid car that can run on two types of fuel—sugar or fat.

When you're younger, your body switches between these fuel sources effortlessly. Carbs for quick energy. Fat stores when you need sustained fuel.

But after 40—especially during perimenopause and menopause—something shifts.

Your body gets "stuck" in sugar-burning mode.

And when you're constantly burning sugar for fuel, three things happen:

  1. You experience afternoon energy crashes (because sugar burns fast and leaves you empty)
  2. Evening cravings become overwhelming (your body is desperately searching for more quick fuel)
  3. Stubborn belly fat refuses to budge (because your body never gets the signal to tap into fat stores)

This is what I call "Metabolic Rigidity."

And if you're a woman over 40 who's been doing "everything right"—eating healthy, staying active, drinking your water—yet still struggling with that frustrating midsection weight and zero energy by 3pm...

This is very likely what's happening inside your body right now.

What Is "Metabolic Rigidity" And Why Does It Happen After 40?

Dr. Holloway explained it to me like this:

When you're younger, your body is metabolically flexible. It switches easily between burning carbs (sugar) and burning fat depending on what you eat and what you need.

But as we age—especially women during perimenopause and menopause—our hormones shift.

  • Insulin sensitivity decreases.
  • Cortisol (the stress hormone) stays elevated longer.
  • And your body starts favoring sugar as its primary fuel source... because it's easier and faster.

The problem?

Sugar burns fast. It gives you a quick spike, then crashes you hard.

And when your body is stuck in sugar-burning mode, it never gets the signal to tap into your fat stores.

So that stubborn belly fat? Those love handles? That softness around your hips? Your body is literally ignoring them as a fuel source.

And no amount of calorie cutting or cardio is going to change that—because you're not addressing the real problem. You're trying to lose fat while your body is stuck burning sugar.

Ketones are natural molecules your body produces when it burns fat for fuel. They're like a "premium fuel" for your brain and muscles—clean, steady, no crashes.

But when your metabolism is rigid and stuck in sugar-burning mode, your body isn't making enough ketones on its own.

That's where exogenous ketones come in.

Exogenous just means "from outside your body."

So instead of forcing yourself into a strict, no-carb diet to produce ketones naturally (which is hard, unsustainable, and honestly miserable), you can take a supplement that provides your body with ketones directly.

And when you do that—when you give your body that gentle metabolic signal every morning—something shifts.
